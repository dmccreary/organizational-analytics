# References: Capstone Projects and Integration

1. [Software Design Pattern](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_design_pattern) - Wikipedia - Overview of reusable solution templates for common software engineering problems. Applicable to designing the modular, reusable graph query libraries and pipeline components assembled in capstone projects.

2. [API (Application Programming Interface)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/API) - Wikipedia - Covers API design principles, REST conventions, and integration patterns. Relevant to building graph analytics services that integrate with existing organizational systems through standardized interfaces.

3. [Continuous Improvement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continual_improvement_process) - Wikipedia - Frameworks (PDCA, Kaizen) for iteratively improving processes and systems. Applicable to establishing ongoing organizational analytics programs that evolve with changing business needs.

4. The Hidden Power of Social Networks - Rob Cross and Andrew Parker - Harvard Business Review Press (2004) - The concluding chapters describe how to sustain organizational network analysis as an ongoing practice, embedding it into leadership decision-making and organizational design processes.

5. Network Science - Albert-László Barabási - Cambridge University Press (2016) - Provides the comprehensive theoretical foundation for understanding organizational network dynamics at scale, supporting the advanced analysis techniques integrated in capstone projects.

6. [Digital Twin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_twin) - Wikipedia - Virtual replicas of physical systems used for simulation and analysis. Emerging concept in organizational analytics where graph-based digital twins model the organization for scenario planning and change simulation.

7. [Benchmarking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benchmarking) - Wikipedia - Systematic comparison of processes and metrics against standards or peers. Relevant to establishing organizational health score baselines and tracking improvement over time.

8. [Health Score (business)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Customer_health_score) - Wikipedia - Composite metrics aggregating multiple indicators into a single measure. Conceptual framework adapted for organizational health scores combining network connectivity, engagement, and knowledge distribution.

9. [AI-Generated Content](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_intelligence_art) - Wikipedia - Overview of AI-generated content detection challenges. Relevant to the capstone topic of identifying AI-authored content within organizational event streams to maintain data provenance.

10. [Systems Integration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/System_integration) - Wikipedia - Methods for linking disparate IT systems into a cohesive whole. Provides context for the end-to-end pipeline integration that connects HR systems, communication platforms, graph databases, and analytics dashboards.
